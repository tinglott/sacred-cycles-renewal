Sacred Cycles: How I Built a Wellness Program That Heals the Whole Person

👩‍⚕️
T. Lott, RN
Registered Nurse · 18 Years Psychiatric Experience · Creator, Sacred Cycles Renewal
April 2026 · 15 min read

It was 2:47 AM on a Tuesday when I had what I can only describe as a reckoning. I was charting at the nurses' station after a brutal twelve-hour psychiatric shift — one that had included a code, a suicide attempt I'd helped pull someone back from, and three hours of holding the emotional weight of a teenager who had no one else. I was exhausted in the bone-deep way that sleep doesn't fix.

I looked at my hands — the same hands that had held needles, administered medications, and pressed call buttons — and I thought: Who heals the healers? After 18 years of absorbing the suffering of people who couldn't afford proper care, of being the last line of defense for the most vulnerable, I was depleted in ways I didn't have clinical language for yet.

That night became the seed of Sacred Cycles Renewal. Not born from a business plan or a marketing strategy, but from the raw recognition that mainstream psychiatry — as powerful as it is — could not hold all of what humans need to truly heal. My patients needed medication, yes. But they also needed ritual. Sound. Touch. Breath. Spirit. They needed to feel that their healing was sacred, not just clinical.

I spent the next three years researching every healing modality I could find — studying the neuroscience of sound therapy, the clinical applications of breathwork, the emerging research on crystal vibration and placebo effect, the ancient wisdom of acupressure and meridian theory. I tested everything on myself first. What worked, I kept. What didn't, I discarded. The result is an 8-week program that integrates five carefully selected healing modalities into one cohesive, clinically-informed wellness experience.

Sacred Cycles is not a rejection of conventional medicine. It's its completion. It's the recognition that healing is a whole-person, whole-life endeavor — and that every person deserves access to it, regardless of their insurance status or zip code. That's why I built this. That's why I keep going.
